## The Story

It started as a simple idea — what if you played as a goblin who just wanted to fish?

No grand quests, no world-ending threats. Just a little green guy on a dock, casting his line into the water on a quiet afternoon. The sun moves across the sky, the water ripples, and for a moment everything feels peaceful.

Then you miss the catch. And suddenly you're the one underwater.

That's the twist at the heart of **Hook, Line and Sinker** — a cozy pixel art fishing game that flips the script. Fail the fishing minigame and the roles reverse. Now you're the fish, darting through a chaotic underwater world, dodging a jellyfish, a shark, an anglerfish, an eel and an octopus — all trying to ruin your day. Survive for 60 seconds and you earn your way back to the dock. The cycle continues.

## How It Was Made

Hook, Line and Sinker was built entirely in **Unity 6** using **C#**. 
The world uses assets from some incredible free packs:

- The **goblin character** comes from the Sunnyside World Asset Pack — a beautifully detailed top-down RPG pack with animations for everything from idle to casting to reeling to catching
- The **fish player** is the adorable clownfish from [AshenThrone's Fishie Sprite pack](https://ashenthrone.itch.io/fishie-sprite) — featuring 6-directional swim animations, ouch reactions and turning frames
- The **underwater enemies** — octopus, jellyfish, shark, anglerfish and eel — come from [Free Game Assets](https://free-game-assets.itch.io/octopus-jellyfish-shark-and-turtle-free-sprite-pixel-art)
- The **underwater parallax background** is from [Ansimuz's Underwater Fantasy pack](https://ansimuz.itch.io/underwater-fantasy-pixel-art-environment)
- The **world tiles, UI and dock** are built from the Sprout Lands UI and Sunnyside asset packs
- The **music** is from Cozy Tunes Pro — gentle ambient tracks that shift depending on whether you're fishing, roaming or underwater
- **Sound effects** come from the JDSherbert Wooden UI SFX Pack and custom sourced fishing sounds


The bubble transition between scenes is taken directly from a classic Spongebob-style wipe, converted to a sprite sheet and animated in engine.
